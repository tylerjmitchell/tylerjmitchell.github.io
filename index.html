Tyler J. Mitchell

About

I am a Ph.D. candidate working with Dr. Paul Knapp in the Carolina Tree-Ring Science Laboratory at UNC Greensboro.

In my work I use tree-ring data to analyze historic variability in climate and weather. I am particularly interested in precipitation–growth responses of pine species at various spatial and temporal scales.

So far, I have been involved with projects that examine tree radial growth responses to specific types (here, here) and intensities (here) of precipitation. I also have been involved with climate reconstructions of specific types of precipitation, including tropical cyclone precipitation during 1750–2015 in coastal NC, USA (here) and quasi-stationary precipitation in central NC, USA during 1790–2018 (here).

A complete list of publications is available by visiting either my Google Scholar or ORCID profile. A current CV is available here.
